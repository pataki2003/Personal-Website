When does a custom website make sense?

If your current website is slow, generic, hard to expand, or simply unclear about what you offer, a stronger structure can make the whole experience easier to understand.

A custom website works best when it supports a real business goal: inquiries, consultations, trust building, or a focused presentation of a service.

Focus

  • Business websites and service landing pages
  • Mobile-friendly, fast frontend implementation
  • Clear content hierarchy
  • Contact paths and calls to action

Process

A clean development flow

1. Goal and offer clarity

We clarify who the website is for, what questions it must answer, and which contact action counts as success.

2. Structure and implementation

The responsive HTML/CSS/JavaScript site is built with stable layout, clear sections, and technical SEO basics.

3. Publishing checks

Before launch, I check mobile views, metadata, links, performance, and the inquiry path.
